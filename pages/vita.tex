\vita{Nathan Dzbenski received a Bachelor of Science degree in physics from the University of North Carolina Wilmington (Wilmington, NC) in 2014. The same year he started graduate school at Old Dominion University (Norfolk, VA), where he received a Masters of Science degree in physics (Nuclear Physics) in 2016. In 2016, he began the Ph.D. program in physics (Experimental Nuclear Physics) at Old Dominion University (Norfolk, VA). The projected date of graduation is August 2020. Nathan Dzbenski is a member of the American Physical Society, CLAS (CEBAF Large Acceptance Spectrometer) collaboration, and CLAS12 Deep Processes Physics Working Group.}
